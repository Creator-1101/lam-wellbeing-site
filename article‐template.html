How to Align Your Root Chakra

Published: June 10, 2025
Author: Mimi Saha
Read Time: 8 min
Category: Spiritual Healing

In this article, we’ll explore how Reiki energy can help you align your Muladhara (Root) chakra, grounding you more deeply in the Earth element. Below you’ll find practical exercises, meditations, and insights to support your journey.

Why the Root Chakra Matters

The Root Chakra is the foundation of our energetic system. When it’s balanced, we feel secure, safe, and supported. When out of balance, we may experience anxiety, restlessness, or physical discomfort in the lower back or legs.

Signs of an Unbalanced Root Chakra

  • Constant worry or fear about survival/finances
  • Lower back pain or tightness in the legs
  • Difficulty staying present—frequent daydreaming
  • Feelings of being “ungrounded” or spacey

Reiki Exercises to Ground Your Root Chakra

  1. Root Chakra Scan: Sit comfortably, place hands on the base of the spine, visualize red light flowing down to your feet. Breathe deeply for 3–5 minutes.
  2. Nature Walk Meditation: Walk barefoot (or in socks on grass) and feel the earth beneath you. Every step, imagine roots growing from your feet. Continue for 10 minutes.
  3. Reiki Energy Placement: With Reiki energy activated (hands in gassho), place your right palm lightly on the pubic bone area (Muladhara) for 5 minutes. Then, place left palm over right to send energy deeper.
“When the Root Chakra is balanced, every cell in your body feels supported by the Earth. You stand tall, breathe deeply, and move with confidence.” – Mimi Saha

Integrating This Practice into Your Daily Life

You don't need to spend hours each day focused on your chakras. Even 5 minutes of seated Reiki practice with a Root Chakra meditation can make a tangible difference. Try the following routine:

Daily 5‐Minute Grounding Routine

  • First thing in the morning, sit cross‐legged for 2 minutes.
  • Place hands on the Root Chakra and breathe gently.
  • Recite the affirmation: “I am safe. I am grounded. I am here.”
  • Visualize red light filling your spine on the exhale.
  • Finish with 3 slow, deep breaths, feeling gratitude.

Over time, these small moments add up. Your body remembers the pattern of being grounded, and you carry that stability into your daily tasks—whether at work, home, or in a social setting.
